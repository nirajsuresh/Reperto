REPERTO Product Requirements — One-Pager
v0.1  ·  March 2026  ·  Draft
Initial scope: Pianists  ·  Platform: Web + iPad PWA
● What
Product
Reperto
The complete platform for a classical musician's journey
Problem
Classical musicians manage their entire catalog in their heads — no purpose-built tool exists for repertoire tracking, practice logging, piece discovery, and community in one place. Existing tools (ForScore, spreadsheets, Reddit) are fragmented, un-connected, and generic.
Approach
A single, unified platform drawing on the best of:
📚 Goodreads → Catalog 🏃 Strava → Practice 🎼 IMSLP → Scores 👥 Strava → Community
Built specifically for classical musicians — not adapted from fitness or books.
Core Features
🗂️
The Shelf
7-stage status pipeline from "Want to Learn" → "Performance Ready"
🎵
Sheet Music Viewer
IMSLP auto-link + PDF annotations, iPad-first via PWA
📊
Practice Analytics
Sessions, hours, comfort ratings, maintenance reminders
🔍
Discovery
Personalised "what to learn next" + repertoire gap analysis
🤝
Community
Connections, groups, challenges, recording sharing
Out of Scope (v1)
Non-piano instruments Native app BT page pedal Video hosting Studio billing
● Who
Primary Persona
The Serious Amateur
Structured goals · 10–20+ yrs playing · occasional performance · wants accountability
Demographics
25–45 yrs · working professional · practices 30–60 min daily · has performance ambitions
Attitude
Motivated and self-directed · frustrated by lack of structure · wants to improve measurably
Needs & Challenges
No peer group at their level · tools are fragmented · stagnation on familiar pieces · no progress visibility
Goals
Build a strong catalog · prepare for performance · connect with similar musicians
How Reperto Helps
Repertoire shelf Practice analytics Peer accountability Level-appropriate discovery Groups & challenges
Secondary Personas
The Hobbyist
Plays for joy · large passive catalog · no performance ambitions
Needs: Discovery, catalog org, inspiration
Risk: forcing practice tracking they'll resist
The Student
Teacher-assigned rep · exam prep (ABRSM, RCM) · clear grade goals
Needs: Progress tracking, grade-level discovery
Risk: over-designing for self-direction
The Semi-Pro
Performing musician · program planning · long-horizon catalog
Needs: Program planner, pro network, perf log
Risk: making features feel too basic
📋 Survey Research (n=24)
Validates
  • ✓ "In my head" is the dominant paradigm
  • ✓ ForScore solves scores, not catalog
  • ✓ Community is fragmented
  • ✓ Stagnation is a real pain point
Watch Out For
  • ! Hobbyists resist practice logging
  • ! Social must be fully opt-in
  • ! Strava gamification feels wrong
  • ! Practice is private by nature
● Why
Short-Term Goals
  • Replace the "I keep it all in my head" paradigm with a purpose-built platform
  • Become the daily home base for serious amateur pianists
  • Prove the IMSLP integration as a zero-friction score delivery model
  • Launch community features that feel personal, not performative
Long-Term Goals
  • The definitive platform for classical musicians across all instruments
  • Institutional tier for conservatories, music schools, and studios
  • A discovery engine that surfaces the right piece at the right moment in a musician's development
  • The world's richest dataset on how people learn classical music

"Reperto is the only platform where your shelf knows your music — scores auto-link from IMSLP, recommendations know your level, and your community knows your journey."
